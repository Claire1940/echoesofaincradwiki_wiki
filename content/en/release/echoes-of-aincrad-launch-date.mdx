<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Demo Save Data Transfer Guide">

If you have already spent time in the Echoes of Aincrad demo, you don't have to worry about losing your progress. Bandai Namco has confirmed that save data from the demo can be carried over to the full game once the **Echoes of Aincrad launch date** arrives. This transfer includes your customized avatar, weapon experience, and progress through the first five missions of the story's beta phase.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update the Game">
    Ensure your full version of Echoes of Aincrad is updated to the latest version on your console or PC.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Detect Save Data">
    Launch the full game. It will automatically scan your system for existing demo save files.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Confirm Carry Over">
    When the prompt appears, select "Carry over." You must press and hold the confirm button to finalize the process.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Start Adventure">
    Select "Game Start" from the title menu and choose your transferred save slot to continue exactly where you left off.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Cross-Platform Limitation">
Save data transfer only works within the same platform ecosystem (e.g., PS5 Demo to PS5 Full Game). You cannot transfer a Steam demo save to a PlayStation 5 console.
</Callout>

It is important to keep the demo save data on your device until the transfer is successfully completed. Deleting the demo or its save data before the full game is launched will result in the loss of all progress.

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Death Game Mode and Survival Rules">

One of the most unique features available at the **Echoes of Aincrad launch date** is the **Death Game Mode**. This optional survival mode is designed to replicate the high-stakes environment of the original Sword Art Online story. In this mode, if your character dies in the game, your save data is permanently erased.

While this mode typically unlocks after completing the main story, owners of the Deluxe and Ultimate Editions can access it from the very beginning.

| Rule Category | Death Game Mode Mechanic |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Permadeath** | Save data is erased upon in-game defeat. |
| **Difficulty** | Can be combined with any difficulty setting. |
| **Save Safety** | Exiting to the title screen or power loss does not delete data. |
| **Unlock Method** | Clear the game or use Early Unlock from Deluxe/Ultimate. |
| **Demo Transfer** | Demo saves cannot be converted into Death Game Mode. |

<Callout type="warning" title="High Risk Warning">
Death Game Mode is intended for experienced players. We recommend completing at least a few floors in the standard mode to master the combat mechanics before attempting a one-life run.
</Callout>

Because your save data is at risk, every choice regarding weapon builds, partner synergy, and item preparation becomes significantly more important. Players must rely on their partners—like Iori or Argo—to manage enemy focus and provide healing during critical moments.
