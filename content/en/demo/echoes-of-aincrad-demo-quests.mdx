<Section icon={Swords} title="Weapon Classes and Sword Skills">

In the world of Aincrad, your identity is defined by your steel. The demo features six distinct weapon types, each offering a unique playstyle and a set of unlockable **Sword Skills**. Unlike traditional RPGs, you do not select a class at character creation; instead, you earn Weapon Exp by defeating enemies with specific arms. You can equip up to three Sword Skills at any time, allowing for significant customization in how you approach combat.

| Weapon Type | Combat Style | Defensive Option | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sword and Shield** | Balanced | Shield Block | Versatile for beginners/solo play |
| **Two-Handed Axe** | Aggressive | None | High AOE damage/Crowd control |
| **Dagger** | Fast/Agile | Ranged Throw | Rapid hits and high mobility |
| **Rapier** | Graceful | Shield Compatible | Excellent for chain attacks/counters |
| **Two-Handed Sword** | Heavy Hitter | Dodge Roll | Massive charged damage output |
| **Mace** | Impact | Shield Compatible | High stagger/Breaking defenses |

Mastering these weapons requires understanding their stamina (SP) consumption. Every action, from light attacks to parries, drains your SP. Managing this resource is the difference between a successful quest and a premature exit from the dungeon. When you reach the Town of Beginnings, visit the Smithy to upgrade your weapon parameters, which is separate from your proficiency level.

<Callout type="info" title="Weapon Management">
You can only change your equipped weapon while at the Inn in the Town of Beginnings. Ensure you have the correct loadout before embarking on a new quest, as you will be locked into that choice until the mission is complete.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Character Progression and Cardinal Rank">

Progression in the **echoes of aincrad demo quests** is dual-tracked through traditional leveling and the unique Cardinal Rank system. While character levels grant Growth Points for stat allocation, your Cardinal Rank serves as a global indicator of your proficiency. This rank dictates the quality of items available at merchants and the difficulty of the monsters you encounter in the open field.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Earn Growth Points">
    Defeat monsters in the dungeon to increase your character level and receive Growth Points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Allocate Stats at the Inn">
    Return to your room at the Inn to spend points on Strength, Dexterity, Agility, Intelligence, Vitality, Endurance, or Mind.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Increase Cardinal Rank">
    Complete Main Quest objectives or discover new towns to raise your rank, unlocking better Smithy recipes and shop items.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade at the Smithy">
    Use materials gathered during quests to level up weapon parameters and combine EX-Mods for enhanced performance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Stat Name | Primary Effect | Recommended For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Increases raw physical damage | Two-Handed Swords / Axes |
| **Agility** | Boosts attack speed and evasion | Daggers / Rapiers |
| **Dexterity** | Enhances critical hit rate and accuracy | Rapiers / Bows (Future) |
| **Vitality** | Increases maximum Health Points (HP) | All Builds (Tank Focus) |
| **Endurance** | Increases maximum Stamina Points (SP) | Heavy Weapon Users |

<Callout type="warning" title="Scaling Difficulty">
As your Cardinal Rank increases, monsters will become significantly tougher. If you fail to upgrade your weapons at the Smithy regularly, you may find yourself underpowered even if your character level is high.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Tactics and Partner Synergy">

Survival in the demo's final quests is nearly impossible without leveraging your partners. Each AI companion brings a specific fighting style and "Combination Skills" that can be triggered via the action wheel. Understanding how to command your partners is vital for managing large groups of enemies or focusing down a single high-threat boss.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Switch Mode">
    - **Focus**: Defensive distraction.
    - **Effect**: Your partner draws enemy aggro.
    - **Best Use**: When you need breathing room to charge a heavy attack or heal.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Free Mode">
    - **Focus**: Offensive coordination.
    - **Effect**: Partners attack independently or focus your target.
    - **Best Use**: Clearing out large groups of low-level mobs efficiently.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Combat is a dance of SP management. You must learn the reach and ability patterns of every new foe. For instance, the spider-type enemies in the demo often use surprise attacks from the ceiling. Using a Mace to stagger them or a Dagger to hit them from range are both viable strategies, provided you coordinate with your team's "Switch" maneuvers.

<Callout type="success" title="Combination Skills">
Assign your partner's Combination Skills to your quick-access wheel. These moves deal massive damage and can often interrupt enemy ultimate attacks, providing a crucial window for a counter-offensive.
</Callout>
