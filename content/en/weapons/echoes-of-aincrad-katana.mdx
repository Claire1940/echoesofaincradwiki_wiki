<Section icon={Swords} title="Understanding the Katana in Echoes of Aincrad">

In the vast, unforgiving floating castle of Aincrad, combat is a test of survival where your chosen gear serves as your class. Unlike traditional MMORPGs, there are no spellcasters or magical powers. Instead, players build expertise and unlock unique **Sword Skills** by repeatedly using specific weapon types. 

Among the community, the **echoes of aincrad katana** stands out as one of the most highly anticipated weapon archetypes. Known in Sword Art Online lore as a high-tier, specialized branch of the curved sword proficiency, the katana represents speed, precision, and devastating counter-attacking playstyles.

<Section icon={Target} title="Unlock Mechanics & Progression Path">

Because the **echoes of aincrad katana** is treated as an advanced specialty weapon rather than a basic starting option, players must progress through specific prerequisites to unlock its corresponding Sword Skills and weapon blueprints. 

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Master the One-Handed Sword">
    Begin by leveling your basic One-Handed Sword proficiency. This builds your fundamental understanding of slash distances, animation frames, and parry timing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Invest heavily in Dexterity (DEX)">
    Katana weapon scaling and unlock parameters require a high baseline of DEX. Allocate your stat points into Dexterity to widen your perfect dodge and parry windows.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Acquire Curved Blade Blueprints">
    Locate and complete Floor Bosses or high-tier dungeon quests to obtain advanced curved sword and katana blueprints at the Town of Beginnings' Smithy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Forge and Equip at the Inn">
    Once the blueprints and materials are gathered, forge your weapon and equip it via the weapon selection menu inside the Inn before heading out on your next quest.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Restriction">
You can only swap your active weapon types while resting in town or at the Inn. Once you enter a dungeon floor or start a quest, your weapon loadout is locked for the duration of that run.
</Callout>

</Section>
